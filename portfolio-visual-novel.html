About

An SFU school group project for my Narrative and New Media class, which for our multi-linear narrative we decided to create an interactive visual novel called Deception in Disguise. In the game, the player controls a high school student trying to uncover a murder that took place in their school a year prior. This is done by questioning their fellow classmates during a Halloween school event.

My Role

My main role was drawing the finalized art of the six characters in the story, two of which I designed myself. I also co-developed the narrative script and was in charge of programming the script into the game.

The detective progonist Blake designed by Percy Bradley.

The machivious prankster Alice designed by Percy Bradley.

The helpful assistant ghost Jesse designed by Annette C.

The charming party boy Charles designed by Samantha B.

The tired and introverted Luciel designed by Shirley L.

The peppy trackstar Mika designed by Alex K.

After group discussion about what characters would be in the story and their characteristics, it was my job to draw them. While several of my classmates wanted to design specific characters it was my job to create both the intuitive protagonist costumed as a detective and one of the prime suspects dressed as a clown named Alice.
After receiving rough art from my group of the other characters I did a quick sketch of how the characters would look together. This was done to make changes to each of their design so they would not look too similar and so that they would have a matching overall colour scheme. I decided to focus mainly on blues and reds to fit with the game’s mainly purple backgrounds

Concept

Deception in Disguise is a parallel narrative combines text-based dialogue, computer graphics and sound. The game is in a visual novel format written in a first-person pov where you must investigate suspects within 3 investigation rounds. There are three possible endings based on the accumulated affinity points gained through the selectable conversation options.

At the start of the project I designed concept for the detective protagonist Blake and one of the suspects Alice. With my group I also documented the game’s mechanics and plot outline on a shared Google Doc.

I wrote the dialogue of all scenes including Alice as well as the game’s introductory scene. During this time I also finished roughly half of the complete illustrations.

I completed the remaining illustrations and lead the coding using Ren’Py I implemented the script into the game, making it so the characters emoting differently with each line. Likewise, I taught my classmates how to use the program.

Challenges

Overall, the main issue for our group was scoupe, we wanted to create something way bigger than what we were assigned and we didn't have enough time.If I had more time I would really have wanted to write more scenes to give the help characterize the characters as well as be able to code more into the game such as audio and visual effects.
This was a project filled with a lot of passion that I wasn't just making with my fellow classmates, but my good friends. When it came to illustrating the characters I was a bit worried about differing their likenesses. If I had to do the project again I would love to experiement more with character's shape language to help better protray their personalities visually. Likewise I'd want to redraw the portraits to be more detailed and have differing poses to better protray emotion in scenes.
