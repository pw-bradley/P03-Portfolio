About

The Skoden Indigenous Film Festival (SIFF) is a Vancouver-based event dedicated to showcasing Canadian Indigenous creators, celebrating their voices, and fostering active discussion about reconciliation. Organized through a group-based course at Simon Fraser University, I played a key role in the festivalâ€™s marketing committee, crafting compelling social media posts to elevate its online presence on Instagram, Facebook and Twitter.
Tasked with revamping the brand for 2023, we retained the iconic logo while redesigning the brand's aesthetic from scratch. Creating new social media banners, posters, and other promotional materials. This dynamic project allowed me to contribute to a meaningful cause through effective visual communication.

My Role

As the lead designer for SIFF 2023, I spearheaded group meetings to establish the logotype, color schemes, and our overarching social media template. Taking a hands-on approach, I crafted numerous posts myself and actively provided feedback and assistance to teammates, ensuring visual cohesion across all designs.

Milestones

Design Ideation

Initiating the project, we utilized concept boards on Canva to explore previous years' student work and draw inspiration. We discussed potential fonts, logotypes, and color schemes.

Early branding concepts created by the design team presented to the class for opinions.

Early branding concepts used together as examples presented in class.

Recognizing the diverse skill levels within the open course I ensured that all voices were heard in the group while providing guidance on hierarchy and design principles. After narrowing down our options we presented potential concepts to our class.

Refined Design Document

Building on the feedback from the previous week, we embraced the casual and friendly aesthetic of our sans-serif concept. As a class, we opted for an orange to green gradient color scheme as our primary colors. Additionally, we decided on four different color schemes depending on the post type. While initially daunting, I proposed limiting the overall colors, resulting in all four schemes blending together to form a rainbow effect.

Social media colour concepts.

An unexpected benefit of this choice emerged when Squamish Nation Elder, Xwechtaal Dennis Joseph, noted that the use of the four color schemes reminded him of the Four Directions on The Medicine Wheel, used by various Indigenous groups.

Refined Logotype and Banner

Responding to critique from the SFU staff social media team, we refined our logotype for a distinct look. Due to software constraints between group members, I exported different layers as PNG files for collaboration in Canva, creating a template guide for consistency.

Posts are Live

We created our posts in mind to be quickly digestible while not diluting information about how colonialism effects Canada. In a usual post I would receive the full text or rough notes of what should be told. Then I found the images for the post and began constructing visuals following our design document.
Throughout the semester, our professors had the final say to ensure accurate information was presented to prevent misconstrued information.

Challenges

A significant challenge revolved around the persistent time constraints we faced. Organizing film festivals of this scale typically spans the year, but our semester-based project intensified the workload within a tight schedule.
This resulted in receiving messages during weekends with less than 24 hours to design a post. To mitigate the stress caused by these time crunches, I played a key role in organizing group schedules and enhancing communication within SIFF the team.
