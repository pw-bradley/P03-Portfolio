About

The Skoden Indigenous Film Festival (SIFF) is a Vancouver-based film festival created to showcase Canadian Indigenous creators, celebrate their voices and cultivate active discussion about reconciliation. The festival is run and organized via a group-based course at Simon Fraser University. Social Media posts were organized by class' marketing committee which I was a key part of.
Our instructors wanted us to create a new brand design from scratch for 2023 compared to SIFF’s previous years. The only design they wanted to keep the same was their logo which has been used throughout the years.

These social media posts were to be put on Instagram, Facebook and Twitter. To adapt to their formats we maintained a square format throughout them all. Social media banners and posters also had to be designed.

My Role

I took the role of lead designer when it came to SIFF's social media posts. I led group meetings to discuss about logotype, colour schemes and our overall social media template. In the team I designed numerous posts myself as well as gave feedback and assisted teammate’s posts to make sure all designs matched each other visually.

Milestones

MILESTONE 01 — Design Ideation

We initiated work by creating concept boards on Canva looking at what students did in previous years and finding work which inspired us. Likewise, we made boards and discussed possible fonts, colours schemes.

Early branding concepts created by the design team presented to the class for opinions.

Early branding concepts used together as examples presented in class.

Being an open course a lot of the design team didn’t have much experience so I took the role as the design team’s leader. I wanted to make sure that all voices were heard while also teaching my groupmates about the importance of hierarchy and design. With this, we whittled down our options and showed these potential options to our class

MILESTONE 02 — Refined Design Document

Based on feedback we received the week prior, we were told to continue forward with our more casual and friendly vibe our blue concept had with its sans-serif interface.
As a class collective we decided to use the colours scheme based on Minty C.’s ideations as our main brand colours. As well as to have a total of four different colour schemes. This was somewhat daunting at first but I came up with the concept where all four colour schemes blended together to make a rainbow.

Social media colour concepts.

A happy accident from this choice that our recurring guest Squamish Nation Elder, Xwechtaal Dennis Joseph mentioned to us was that the four colour schemes reminded him of the Four Directions as seen on The Medicine Wheel used by various Indigenous tribes.

MILESTONE 03 — Refined Logotype and Banner

After receiving some critique from our SFU staff social media team, we reworked our logotype to be more unique and interesting. Due to our team not owning the Adobe Suite I exported the different layers of the posts as PNG files that could be layered together in Canva which everyone could access. With this I also created a template guide to follow.

MILESTONE 04 — Posts are Live

We created our posts in mind to be quickly digestible while not diluting information about how colonialism effects Canada. In a usual post I would receive the full text or rough notes of what should be told. Then I found the images for the post and began designing following our template.
Throughout the semester we made sure to give the final say to our professors so that our information was presented correctly without accidentally misconstruing information.

Challenges

A big challenge I felt was the constant time crunch we were under. Which makes sense since our professors told us that usually film festival such as these were organized through the year instead of within a single semester. This meant that the work schedule was very tight and had to be made in very short notice.
Early into posting, there was an instance where I’d receive messages during the weekend telling me I had under 24 hours to design a post. After that, I helped organize the groups’ schedules and communication throughout the team to make time crunch less stressful.
